# Light Novel Style Guide

Use this guide whenever prose quality matters more than broad planning.

## Narrative Priorities

1. Start scenes late.
2. Make the protagonist want something concrete in every scene.
3. Let dialogue reveal character before exposition explains them.
4. End scenes with a shift, reveal, or emotional sting.

## Voice

- Favor clean, contemporary Japanese.
- Keep narration emotionally close to the viewpoint character.
- Let reactions be specific rather than generic.
- Use humor, embarrassment, bravado, or tension in quick beats when it fits the cast.

## Sentence And Paragraph Rhythm

- Mix short lines with medium descriptive lines.
- Break paragraphs before the page feels heavy.
- Use emphasis sparingly; the moment should earn it.

## Character Writing

- Give each major character a recognizable speech pattern or value system.
- Let conflict appear in banter, not only in plot summaries.
- Keep side characters useful: foil, ally, rival, witness, or catalyst.

## Scene Construction

- Open with motion, trouble, or a charged exchange.
- Anchor the scene with one or two vivid details.
- Escalate once before the scene ends.
- Leave the reader wanting the next paragraph, not just the next chapter.

## Common Failure Modes

- Explaining the world before the reader cares
- Repeating the same emotional reaction three times
- Flattening all characters into the same speaking voice
- Spending too long on neutral travel, meals, or setup with no tension
- Turning every climax into summary instead of scene
